EcodEX is a eco-design software tool for evaluating and understanding the environmental impacts of packaging products.

Designed to be used by non-experts, it helps companies improve the sustainability of new products or the existing portfolio, in a cost-effective and simple way.

  • Certified to ISO 14040-14044
  • Enables a 360 degree view of your product
  • Used by leading global companies
  • Empowers decision making
  • Supported globally by leading software developers

Extensive Feature Set

EcodEX follows a multi criteria approach to evaluate the impact of products across;

  • Global Warming Potential
  • Energy Use
  • Land Use
  • Water Consumption
  • Solid Waste
  • Eutrophication
  • Package to Weight Ratio

The flexibility of EcodEX permits indicators to be added and/or removed; aligning reporting to a company’s business goals and strategy.

Simulation & What If Analysis

EcodEX compares various options and performs simulations to determine the optimal improvement to a product. This brings the benefit of being able to simulate changes to products, such as:

  • Simulate changes in the packaging of a product
  • Simulate changes in the ingredients of a recipe
  • Simulate changes in the manufacturing process
  • Simulate supplier or other sourcing changes, like geography or agricultural practices

Strategic Design Support

Consumer goods companies operate in an environment of daunting technical, regulatory and commercial uncertainties. Throughout the business lifecycle, companies now face critical decisions related to sustainability of their products and business.

  • Designed by leading industry experts in consideration of real life commercial practices
  • EcodEX models data within the tool itself, powerful interrogation, clear scientific methodology Removes cumbersome interpretation reports and time delays
  • Powered by EcoInvent Databases

Seamless Integration

Many companies seek to improve the sustainability of their products and business. Research indicates that the integration of lifecycle impact analysis into the new product development cycle can provide substantial benefits towards achieving this goal such as:

  • Simple, quick, cost-effective analysis of the environment impact of an experimental product prior to launch,
  • Evaluation of different packaging design ideas or ingredients, and
  • Screen and make suppliers responsible for the environmental impact of their raw materials.
